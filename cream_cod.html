Portuguese Cream Cod

Ingredients

  • One or two pieces of frozen salted cod, normally found in Portuguese grocers;
  • 1KG Frying Potatoes (Any high starch potato will do), cut into small-medium cubes;
  • 500ml milk;
  • 250ml water used to boil cod;
  • 3 tbsp flour;
  • 1-2 onions;
  • 3-6 garlic cloves, to taste;
  • 50g butter;
  • Nutmeg to taste;
  • Salt to taste.

Steps

  1. In a large pot, cover cod with water and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and leave to cool;
  2. Break cod down, removing any bones and skin. Cod should tear very easily into chunks. Preserve 250ml of the water;
  3. In an appropriate pot, bring oil to temperature and begin to fry your potatoes in batches. Drain them in a bowl with paper towel to remove excess oil;
  4. While your potatoes fry, in a large pot, brown onions in olive oil over low heat. Add garlic once browning starts;
  5. Add butter and, once everything is nice and brown (onion and garlic should be very fragrant at this point), add half of your cod and stir-fry, carefully not to break down the chunks too much;
  6. Cover with the flour and nutmeg and add milk to create a sort of bechamel texture in the pan, add the cod water. Reduce.
  7. Once mixture thickens, lower the heat and add the remainder of the cod, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes as they finish frying (don't forget to drain the excess oil!);
  8. Once everything is in the pot you should have what looks like a thick, creamy potato and cod stew;
  9. Move contents into an oven-appropriate dish, add cream and stir loosely into mixture;
  10. Bake for 20-30 minutes until the top begins to char. Once cooked, allow it to rest for a few minutes to let everything set;
  11. Bon appetit!
